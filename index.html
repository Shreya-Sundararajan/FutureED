Software Developer

💻

What They Do: Software developers design, code, and test computer applications and systems. They work in various industries to create software that meets user needs.

Required Education

Bachelor's degree in computer science, software engineering, or related field. Many developers also pursue certifications in specific technologies.

Skills Needed

  • Programming languages (Python, Java, JavaScript, etc.)
  • Problem-solving and analytical thinking
  • Attention to detail
  • Collaboration and communication
  • Continuous learning mindset

Salary & Job Outlook

Median Salary: $110,140 per year

Job Growth: 22% (2020-2030) - Much faster than average

Entry-Level Education: Bachelor's degree

Free Learning Resources

  • freeCodeCamp - Full coding curriculum
  • Khan Academy - Computer Programming
  • Harvard's CS50 on edX
  • Mozilla Developer Network

Scholarships Available

  • Google Computer Science Scholarship - Up to $10,000
  • Microsoft Scholarship Program - Varies
  • Society of Women Engineers Scholarships - $1,000-$15,000
  • Hispanic Scholarship Fund - Up to $5,000

A Day in the Life

Software developers typically work in offices or remotely, spending their day writing code, debugging programs, meeting with team members, and planning new features. Many work standard 40-hour weeks, though deadlines may require extra hours.

Professional Insight

"One of the most rewarding aspects of being a software developer is seeing your code come to life and actually help people. The field is constantly evolving, so there's always something new to learn, which keeps the work exciting and challenging." - Sarah Johnson, Senior Software Engineer
